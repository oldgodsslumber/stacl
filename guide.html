Welcome Aboard
CAPTAIN'S LOG
A Star Trek Adventures companion for the table. Track your officer, chart the sector, manage NPCs, roll dice, consult the oracle, and keep a mission log — all from one screen.

This guide walks through every tab and feature. Use the arrows below or your keyboard to navigate.

Getting Started
MISSION SLOTS
Captain's Log stores up to three separate missions in your browser. Switch between them with the MISSION 1 / 2 / 3 buttons at the top of the screen.

Use SAVE FILE to export all missions as a single JSON backup, and LOAD FILE to restore from one. This is the safest way to preserve your campaigns long-term.

⚙ Settings button — rename your missions, reset a slot, or delete all data for it.

NIGHT MODE — switches to a red-tinted LCARS theme. Ideal for low-light play.

TAB 1
👨‍✈️
OFFICER
Build and track your player character — from species and attributes through disciplines, focuses, values, talents, and stress. Your ship lives here too.
Character Creation Ship Stats Interior Map
🧬
Officer Tab
CHARACTER CREATION
Step through creation in order: Species → Era → Environment → Upbringing → Track → Career. Each choice adds attribute and discipline bonuses automatically. Once you click Complete Creation, the full officer sheet is revealed.

Attributes (Control, Daring, Fitness, Insight, Presence, Reason) start at 7 and are raised via species, environment, and upbringing choices.

Disciplines (Command, Conn, Security, Engineering, Science, Medicine) represent trained skills and are raised by track and career choices.

Focuses are narrow specialisations. Values are character-defining beliefs that can be challenged or invoked.

🚀
Officer Tab
SHIP & INTERIOR MAP
Select a ship class to auto-fill systems and departments. Give your ship a name, registry, and scale. The vessel profile card shows the class art and trait text.

Ship Interior Map — a 5×10 deck grid. Click any cell to label it, place an emoji icon, or add notes. Void cells (dashed) are outside the hull — click to toggle them into active rooms.

Reset Layout restores the default hull shape for the selected class scale.

TAB 2
🗺️
MAP
Chart the sector, drill down into sub-zones, and track your ship's current position. Every sector is a 5×5 grid of named, iconified cells.
Sector Grid Sub-Zones Cell Details
📡
Map Tab
SECTOR MAP
The main map is a 5×5 sector grid. Click any cell to name it, add an emoji icon, note features, and write observation notes. Your ship's current location is highlighted.

Warp Here — moves your ship to the selected cell and logs it.

Enter Zone — drills down into a sub-zone for that cell (system, station, nebula, etc). Sub-zones are their own 5×5 grids.

Add Sector — create additional sectors for a wider campaign map. Switch between them from the sector list.

🔭
Map Tab
SUB-ZONES
Any sector cell can contain a sub-zone — a zoomed-in 5×5 map for a star system, space station, asteroid field, or any local area of interest.

Select a sector cell → click Enter Zone → choose a type. You'll drop into the sub-zone view with its own grid.

Name each cell, add icons, and track the current location within the zone. Click Exit Zone to return to the sector.

Sub-zones appear in the Export Log MAPS panel for inclusion in your After Action Report.

TAB 3
👥
NPCs
Track every notable character the crew encounters — allies, adversaries, contacts, and unknowns. Each NPC has a full profile and a relationship attitude.
Attitude Tracking Faction & Role Goals & Traits
🎭
NPCs Tab
NPC PROFILES
Each NPC card stores: name, faction, role, attitude, trait, goal, and free notes. Attitude ranges from Hostile → Cautious → Neutral → Allied → Crew, colour-coded for quick reference.

Add NPC opens a quick-entry form. Fill in as much or as little as you know.

NPCs can be spotlit on the HUD — their name and notes appear live on the stream overlay for players watching remotely.

NPCs appear as draggable cards in the Export Log for inclusion in your After Action Report.

TAB 4
🎲
DICE
Roll challenge dice using your character's attributes and disciplines. Track momentum, threat, and complications in real time.
Challenge Dice Momentum Threat Pool Task Tracks
🎯
Dice Tab
ROLLING & TRACKING
Select an Attribute and a Discipline to set your Target Number. Add a focus for a bonus, then roll. Results are colour-coded: Critical (1), Success, Failure, and Complication (20).

Momentum and Threat are tracked at the top — tap +/- to adjust mid-scene.

Extended Tasks — add a task track with custom progress circles. Each filled circle advances the work.

Combat — add opposition or player stress to track a scene's damage state.

All roll results are broadcast live to the HUD stream overlay.

ORACLE
Solo and GM tools. Generate random scenes, complications, threats, and story beats drawn from Star Trek-flavoured word and event tables.
Scene Prompts Word Tables Mission Generator

Oracle Tab
ORACLE TABLES
Choose a table type and roll for a random prompt — complications, motivations, locations, alien threats, and more. Use the results to spark improvisation at the table.

Mission Generator — combine a mission type, complication, and objective for a ready-to-run scenario seed.

Word tables — single evocative words drawn from Trek-flavoured categories to unstick a stuck scene.

Oracle results are pushed live to the HUD stream overlay ticker.

Write, organise, and review your mission log. Every session leaves a paper trail.

Log Tab
WRITING THE LOG
Tap + NEW ENTRY to open the entry form. Choose a type, give it a title, and write. Entries stack newest-first. Scroll to review earlier entries.

Standard — regular captain's log entries.

Supplemental — follow-up entries, indented and coloured differently.

Red Alert — combat or crisis entries, highlighted in warning red.

▶ EPISODE START — inserts an episode break banner then immediately opens a new entry. Use at the start of each session to divide your log into episodes.

STREAMING
📺
HUD STREAM OVERLAY
Broadcast a live LCARS overlay to your stream or video call. The HUD window updates in real time as you play — no refresh needed.
Live Stats Dice Results Maps NPC Spotlight
🖥️
Streaming
USING THE HUD
Click HUD in the top bar (desktop only — requires a wide screen) to open the stream panel. Then click OPEN HUD WINDOW to launch the overlay in a separate window.

Capture the HUD window in OBS, Streamlabs, or your video call screen-share tool. Size it as needed.

Use the HUD Controls panel to toggle which panels are visible: character, ship, sector map, interior map, dice, oracle, mission, log, NPC spotlight, and camera feed.

NPC Spotlight — select an NPC to display their name and notes in a floating panel on the overlay.

Bridge backgrounds — choose a themed bridge image behind the HUD panels.

↗️
Log Tab → Export
BUILDING YOUR REPORT
Click ↗ EXPORT in the log tab header to open the After Action Report in a new tab. The page has three areas:

Source panel (left) — draggable NPCs, maps, and stat cards organised under NPCS / MAPS / STATS tabs.

ANNEXES column — drop source items here to include them alongside the log. Drag the handles to reorder. Click ✕ to remove.

MISSION LOG column — your filtered log entries, rendered automatically. Use the episode checkboxes in the top bar to show/hide specific episodes.

Notes blocks are renameable — click the gold title to edit it.

🖨️
Log Export
PRINTING & SAVING
The export page has two display modes. Toggle between them with the button in the top bar, then use your browser's Print → Save as PDF to archive the report.

Dark mode (default) — full LCARS black background with colour accents. Prints as-is when "Background graphics" is enabled in your browser's print dialogue.

◑ Print Mode — switches to a light grey/white theme optimised for ink saving. Source panel and drag controls are hidden automatically when printing.

In Chrome: File → Print → More settings → enable Background graphics for dark mode output.

⚡
Quick Reference
TIPS & SHORTCUTS
A few things worth knowing before you head to the bridge.

All data is local — nothing is sent to a server. Use SAVE FILE regularly to back up your missions.

Mobile — the full app works on phones. The bottom nav bar replaces the side nav. HUD streaming is desktop-only.

Episode Start — use ▶ EPISODE START instead of the plain episode break. It inserts the banner and opens the entry form in one step.

Night Mode — the red-alert theme is great for late-night play. It persists between sessions.

Export early, export often — build your report after each session while it's fresh.
